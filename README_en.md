## 📖 Overview

In 2026, a U.S. court held social media platforms liable — not for their content, not for their algorithms, but for their **design philosophy itself** — for harming the mental health of children.

The attention economy is ending. But no one yet knows what comes next.

This book is written to draw that "next." It does not present a finished model. **It presents a Design Brief — a set of design conditions.**

Beginning with a structural anatomy of the attention economy, this book traces how courts established the new legal concept of "design liability," the invisible trillion-dollar social costs, the attention tax as a new axis of inequality, the "metrics trap" proven by Facebook's MSI (Meaningful Social Interactions) failure, transition-era experiments and their failures, the redesign of creator compensation, the definition of "true engagement" grounded in Self-Determination Theory (SDT), and culminates in six design conditions for the next-generation social platform.

**This book does not write answers. It writes a blueprint. The person who builds the next SNS might be you, after reading these design conditions.**
